# Strategy through the viral-reframe lens (STEPPS + CC moves)

How the playbook maps onto product + prototype + launch content decisions.

## Lever-by-lever audit of the concept

| Lever | Where it lives in OUR version | Design implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Social Currency** | SC-4 "make access feel earned, not free" → the **vetting application IS the viral mechanic**, not just safety. "Apply to join" outperforms "download free app". | Prototype must show an application, acceptance state, and visible member status. Membership = brag. |
| **Triggers** | Trip-planning moments: booking flight, landing day, "first night alone in a hostel". | Content calendar anchors to these moments; product asks for dates (trigger = "your trip is in 12 days"). |
| **Emotion** | Safety in Colombia = **anxiety** (high-arousal, shareable). Friendship payoff = awe/excitement. Never frame sad/lonely (low-arousal kills shares — anti-pattern #7). | Hooks open on the fear or the flex, never "it can be lonely out here". |
| **Public** | Live traveler counter + avatar stack = P-4 visible residue. "N going to Medellín" grows with every video. | Counter is the screen-recordable artifact. Every application makes the next video's number bigger → content/product flywheel. |
| **Practical Value** | The friend's DM demand: safety + money tips. PV-1: tighten "for travelers" → "for the friend who just booked Medellín". | Vetted community ships a concrete deliverable on join (safety doc / first-72-hours guide) = forwardable utility. |
| **Stories** | ST-2/ST-3: friend is the protagonist; product must be **load-bearing** — if the story works without the app, the app gets stripped in retelling. | His videos can't just *mention* the community — the payoff of each story must route through it ("the guy I met through the group is why this video exists"). |

## CC moves that bind the plan
- **CC-18 Through-line:** every feed post must draw a direct line to the offer; pure travel content goes to stories. This is the content-first pipeline's routing rule.
- **CC-6 Super Hook:** his credential = months on the ground in Colombia + the viral receipts ("9M people watched my Colombia videos").
- **CC-17 Plural Pain Naming:** "you ever land in a city where you know absolutely nobody?" — the launch video's emotional spine.
- **CC-8 High TAM:** hook stays universal (anyone who's been alone somewhere new); niche (Medellín dates, vetting) lives in the value/CTA.
- **CC-14 15-25% pattern interrupt:** vetted-application is the one category convention we break; everything else (group chat, meetups) stays familiar.
- **CC-15 honey pot / SC-6 withhold:** never explain the full vetting criteria publicly — "not everyone gets in" does the work.

## Launch hook candidates (platform: tiktok)

[Stories + Emotion — ST-7 + CC-11]: "9 million people watched my Colombia videos. The DMs all ask the same 3 questions. So my best friend built the answer."

[Social Currency + Public — SC-4 + P-4]: "We made a members-only travel group for Colombia. 217 people applied in week one. Here's what it takes to get in."

[Emotion + Practical Value — E-2(anxiety) + PV-1 + CC-2]: "If you're coming to Medellín alone, 3 things will silently ruin your trip — the third is why we built this."

WHY:
- V1 — receipts as Super Hook, withholds "the answer" (curiosity loop), product is load-bearing.
- V2 — earned access + visible count; the number compounds across videos.
- V3 — negative-framing trust (CC-2) rides the exact anxiety his DMs prove exists.

TRADE-OFF: V1 safest (pure story, zero claims). V2 boldest (needs a real counter — prototype dependency). V3 most defensible utility play and best fit for the safety demand signal.

## Decision this forces on the prototype
The prototype's job is to make **V2-class content possible**: a live, screen-recordable counter + an application flow with an acceptance moment. That means tonight's build = city page (counter + avatar stack) + apply form + "you're in 🎉" state. Map/AI features fail ST-3 (story works without them) — skip.
